Who Is the Mad Dog Alpha — And Why Did She Choose Him?

Everyone in the pack calls him the Mad Dog. They say it like it's an insult. Like it explains why he has nothing — no territory, no standing, no clean name. What they don't say is why he became that way. What was taken from him. Who did the taking.

From the Author

"He was always going to be the one she chose. Not because he was the strongest — he wasn't, not yet. But because he was the only one in that room who looked at her like she was a person and not a prize. She noticed. She always notices the small things."

The Warrior Luna didn't pick the Mad Dog out of desperation. She picked him out of clarity. She looked at three powerful alphas and saw exactly what they would do with her power. Then she looked at him — disgraced, underestimated, burning — and she saw something else entirely. She saw a match.

✍ Author Notes Writing Life

Why I Keep Writing the Omega Who Refuses to Break

People ask me why almost every heroine I write starts at the bottom. The omega. The girl the pack ignores. The one who gets the leftover everything — leftover attention, leftover respect, leftover love.

The honest answer is that those are the stories I want to read. The ones where the person everyone counted out ends up being the most powerful person in the room — not because she was secretly strong all along, but because she decided to be. That decision. That moment. That's what I'm always writing toward.

The Real Reason

"I want the reader to feel what it's like to be underestimated and then watch that girl take everything back. Not just the alpha. Everything. Her dignity. Her name. Her power. I want the reader to feel that like it's happening to them."

The Hybrid Luna — What It Means and Why the Pack Is Terrified

In the world of His Troublemaker Luna, a hybrid Luna is not just rare. She is a rupture in the natural order. The pack hierarchy was built on the idea that wolves are wolves and humans are humans and the lines between them are fixed. A hybrid Luna breaks every single one of those lines.

World Lore

"A hybrid Luna carries both wolf and something older in her blood. She doesn't just shift — she absorbs. She doesn't just bond — she rewrites. The alpha who claims her doesn't gain a mate. He gains a mirror. Whatever he is, she reflects it back twice as bright, twice as sharp, twice as dangerous."

This is why they feared her before they even knew what she was. The pack sensed it. The alpha knew it the first time he looked at her. She was already more powerful than anyone in that pack, including him. She just hadn't found out yet.
