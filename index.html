His Troublemaker Luna

She Was Never Supposed to Be His.

"He let them call her weak. Let his brothers push her around, let the pack treat her like she was nothing — because the moment he claimed her, the moment he said her name out loud in front of everyone, it was over for him. He knew that. So he waited. He watched. He told himself he was being patient. He was lying. There is nothing patient about the way he looked at her. Nothing kind about the way he counted down the days. She was seventeen, untouched, and already the most dangerous thing in his world — and she didn't even know it yet."

The Warrior Luna & Her Mad Dog Alpha

She Did Not Come Home to Be Tamed.

"A hundred battles. A hundred victories. She came home with blood on her hands and her head held high, certain that the title was hers. Her father looked at her and said — you are a woman. As if that explained everything. As if that erased everything. They lined up three Alphas for her to choose from. Strong ones. Safe ones. The kind of men her father could control through her. She looked at all three of them. Then she looked at him — the one they called the Mad Dog, the disgraced one, the lowest of the low. And she smiled. If she was going to burn everything down anyway, she might as well choose the match."
